Posted on Nov 09, 2006
Salinas' Soledad Street has been a refuge for the hungry and homeless for more than two decades. For the past decade, CSUMB has been sending students to Dorothy's Place to do the service learning work that every student at the university is required to do. Over the last five years, students have been putting in a combined 3,000 volunteer hours a year on Soledad Street, says Seth Pollack , director of CSUMB's Service Learning Institute.
– Monterey County Weekly, Nov. 9, 2006
