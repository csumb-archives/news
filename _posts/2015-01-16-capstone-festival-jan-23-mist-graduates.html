--- layout: archive title: Capstone Festival Jan. 23 for MIST graduates date: January 16 2015 ---
Posted on Jan 16, 2015

Deanna Brown’s capstone project will help her fellow students prepare for graduate school. Michael Stewart aims to help recovering addicts maintain sobriety. And Deborah Stephens is teaching community college students how to use the resources of their campus library.

The three will join 10 classmates in presenting their projects at the Capstone Festival for the Master’s in Instructional Science and Technology (MIST) program at Cal State Monterey Bay. The festival will be held from 4:30 to 6:30 p.m., Jan. 23, in Room 118 of the Media Learning Center. Driving directions and a campus map are available at csumb.edu/maps.

The 16-month program prepares students for positions in the fields of education and training. The coursework integrates instructional design, learning theory, information technology and media design.

“I enjoyed the program; it’s been a great experience,” said Callie Chastain, an analyst in CSUMB’s School of Business. “It’s been hugely advantageous for me.” For her capstone, Chastain designed online modules for use by incoming students in the university’s Master of Business Administration program.

Students will receive their degrees at the May 16 commencement ceremony.
