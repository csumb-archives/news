--- layout: archive title: Banners tell farm workers' stories date: May 30 2006 ---
Posted on May 30, 2006
Along North Main Street in Salinas, 10 brightly colored banners hang from atop light poles, each with its own story or message of what it means to be a home owner. The banners, installed in front of the Salinas Sports Complex, are the work of 17 digital public art students at California State University, Monterey Bay. The work is the result of a collaboration between the Department of Visual and Public Art and the Center for Community Advocacy. "In our class, we aim for making art that the public has a role in developing," said Gilbert Neri, a professor in the art department who teaches the public art class.
– Salinas Californian, May 30, 2006
